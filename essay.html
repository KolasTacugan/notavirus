First Monthsary

To my Carl Lorreine,

When I was just about ready to give up on love, the universe had its own quiet way of proving me wrong.

I still remember swiping on Bumble without really knowing what I was looking for. I came across your profile and, half-jokingly, thought to myself that I probably couldn’t handle your attitude—purely based on your pictures. I said “oh well” and swiped right anyway. And I’m so glad I did.

At first, I truly believed I wasn’t capable of getting attached anymore. I told myself I was just talking casually, nothing serious. But somewhere along the way, we fell into a routine, and I started to look for you without even realizing it. I denied it for a while. I had my walls up, convinced this would be another short-lived thing—one of those connections that ends after a couple of weeks when you find a reason to walk away.

But we didn’t.

When I got hospitalized, what hurt most wasn’t being admitted. It was knowing I wouldn’t get to have our usual sleep calls or movie nights. That’s when it hit me—how much you had already become part of my life. I was developing real feelings, even if I was still scared to admit it back then.

You have this way of getting through to me so naturally. With you, I feel safe. Talking to you feels easy—except, of course, when I have to speak in Tagalog. When you first told me you were coming to see me, I panicked. Not because I was hiding anything, but because I was scared that meeting in person might change everything. What if we noticed something we didn’t like?

But it didn’t change things. It only made me love you more.

As cheesy as it sounds, this is the first time I’ve felt truly secure in a relationship. I stopped overthinking. During those seven days, I felt at home, and I couldn’t get enough of spending time with you. Our situation isn’t always easy, but I’m grateful to have someone I can miss—someone who makes distance feel meaningful.

This Valentine’s Day, even if I can’t be there physically, I’d love to go back to how we started. Just us, watching a movie together.

So let me ask you, sincerely:

Will you be my Valentine?
